Drog Tests

A blood drug test measures the presence of legal or illegal substances in your bloodstream. Because drugs are metabolized and eliminated from the blood relatively quickly, blood tests are highly accurate at detecting recent use or current intoxication rather than past history.

Hematology..

Hematology is the study of blood, blood-forming organs, and blood disorders. Clinical hematology laboratories perform diagnostic tests to evaluate cell counts (red cells, white cells, platelets), hemoglobin, and clotting factors to help diagnose conditions like anemia, infections, hemophilia, and leukemia.

Thyroid Panel (TSH, T3, T4)

A thyroid panel (TSH, T3, T4) is a group of blood tests used to evaluate how well your thyroid gland is functioning. These tests diagnose conditions like hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid) and hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid) by measuring hormone production and the communication between your pituitary gland and thyroid.

Liver Function Tests (LFTs)

A group of tests that evaluate the performance and health of the liver. They measure enzymes and proteins produced by the liver, such as: 1] ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) 2] AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) 3] Bilirubin 4] ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase)

Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)

A Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP) is a routine blood test that measures 14 different substances to evaluate your metabolism, fluid balance, and the health of your liver and kidneys. Doctors frequently use it for routine physicals, monitoring chronic diseases like diabetes, or checking for medication side effects

Kidney/Renal Function Tests

A kidney renal function test (RFT) evaluates how effectively your kidneys filter waste and balance fluids. It typically involves blood and urine tests to measure markers like creatinine, BUN, and electrolytes. Regular testing is vital for monitoring chronic conditions like diabetes and high blood pressure.

Circulating Tumor Test(Liquid Biopsy)

A liquid biopsy is a minimally invasive blood test that detects cancer by analyzing circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) or circulating tumor cells (CTCs) shed into the bloodstream. It offers a rapid, painless, and dynamic alternative to traditional surgical tissue biopsies for diagnosing and monitoring cancers
